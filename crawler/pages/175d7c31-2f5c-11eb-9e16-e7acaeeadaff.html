SoftServe Olha Hrytsay SoftServe Presentation at SATURN 2014 Presenters explore reference architectures that address the challenges of Big Data Publisher Software Engineering Institute Watch Abstract If data were equated to temperature in physical analogy according to the second law of thermodynamics its accumulation would lead to the heat death of the universe In fact 90 of the world s data has been generated during the last two years Fortunately business intelligence BI architectures and technologies are evolving rapidly to keep up with the exponential rise of data and project complexity An almost era of dominance for the relational database management system RDBMS ended and there is no solution that solves all of today s Big Data challenges BI architecture drivers must change to satisfy new requirements in format volume latency hosting analysis reporting and visualization In this session we expose a number of reference architectures that address these challenges and speed up the design and implementation process making it more predictable and economical traditional architecture based on an RDMBS data warehouse but modernized with storage to handle a high load and capacity architectures that address Big Data batch and processing and use popular NoSQL and complex solutions hybrid architecture that combines traditional and NoSQL approaches to achieve completeness that would not be possible with either alone The architectures are accompanied by projects and case studies that the presenters have performed for multiple companies including Fortune 100 and Share
