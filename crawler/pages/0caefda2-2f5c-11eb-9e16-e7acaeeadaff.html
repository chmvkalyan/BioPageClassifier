wildland fire community for planning Publisher Software Engineering Institute Abstract This session describes how the interagency Joint Fire Science Program JFSP developed and assessed the Interagency Fuel Treatment Decision Support System IFTDSS to meet the needs of the wildland fire community for planning for wildland fire The past decade saw a dramatic proliferation of software systems intended to help fire and fuels managers These systems were created by many developers and funded by a variety of sources The systems were developed without any central control or vision and deployed without a governance process for transitioning developmental or software applications to operationally ready supported applications While this resulted in an increase in capability it also resulted in a fuels management environment with numerous tools system and data access problems inconsistent fuels management planning minimal and fragmented security and ad hoc training It also resulted in a frustrated fire and fuels management community To address this software chaos JFSP worked extensively with users to incorporate a set of existing tools into the IFTDSS using a approach Prior to the deployment decision JFSP continued their user outreach by conducting an independent assessment of IFTDSS focused on four key areas alignment with enterprise architecture guidance impact of the SOA approach on software development by the community usability by both novice and expert users and impact on training and knowledge management The assessment concluded that IFTDSS could be a major step toward meeting the wildland fire community s strategic goals if fielded as part of a cohesive governance strategy Share Download PDF Part of a Collection Software Solutions Conference SSC 2015 Presentations
