Stories Archives 2014 March Press Release Brian Junker Selected as 2014 American Educational Research Association Fellow March 18 2014 Press Release Brian Junker Selected as 2014 American Educational Research Association Fellow Contact Shilo Rea shilo American Educational Research Association AERA has selected Carnegie Mellon University Brian Junker as a 2014 AERA Fellow Junker professor of statistics and associate dean for academic affairs for the Dietrich College of Humanities and Social Sciences is one of 22 scholars honored this year for notable and sustained research achievements We are pleased to honor these individuals for their significant contributions to education research and to the advancement of the field said AERA Executive Director Felice Levine AERA Fellows exemplify the best of research in terms of accomplishment quality mentoring and the highest professional standards We welcome the class of 2014 to these ranks Junker who came to Carnegie Mellon as a postdoc in 1990 focuses on applications of statistics in education research psychology and the social sciences He has worked in areas including nonparametric and Bayesian item response unfolding and rating models psychometric cognitive diagnosis models and predictive modeling of online tutoring systems census systems statistical analysis of large randomized field trials in education social network analysis and mixed membership models for longitudinal data in epidemiology He is a fellow of the Institute of Mathematical Statistics and served as president of the Psychometric Society and editor of its journal Psychometrika from He is currently of the Design and Analysis Committee for the National Assessment of Educational Progress NAEP and directs a postdoctoral training program for statistics in education research in collaboration with RAND Corporation Pittsburgh office In his role as associate dean Junker chairs the Dietrich College Council and helps lead the college in administering curriculum programs and policies He teaches both undergraduate and graduate level courses within the Department of Statistics advises graduate students and works with postdoctoral researchers Junker and the 2014 AERA Fellows will join 557 current fellows when they are inducted on Friday April 4 during the AERA annual meeting in Philadelphia For more information on Junker visit http CMU Brian
