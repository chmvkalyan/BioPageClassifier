Software Engineering Institute Report Number DOI Digital Object Identifier Subjects Software ArchitectureSoftware Architecture Abstract The Architecture Tradeoff Analysis Initiative at the Carnegie Mellon Software Engineering Institute SEI has developed a number of methods currently in use including the SEI Architecture Tradeoff Analysis Method ATAM the SEI Quality Attribute Workshop QAW the SEI Cost Benefit Analysis Method CBAM SEI Active Reviews for Intermediate Designs ARID and the SEI Design ADD method Building on our success in developing and piloting a collection of software architecture methods we now focusing on integrating them and building the bridges between them and the processes and architecture efforts outside the SEI all the while continuing to refine existing methods and models This technical note reports on a proposal to integrate the SEI ATAM and SEI CBAM The ATAM provides software architects with a framework for understanding the technical tradeoffs and risks they face as they make design decisions but it does not provide any guidance for understanding economic tradeoffs The CBAM helps software architects consider the return on investment of any architectural decision and provides guidance on the economic tradeoffs involved The CBAM takes the architectural decision analysis done during the ATAM and helps make it part of a strategic roadmap for software design and evolution by associating priorities costs and benefits with architectural decisions Share Download PDF Part of a Collection Architecture Tradeoff Analysis Method Collection
