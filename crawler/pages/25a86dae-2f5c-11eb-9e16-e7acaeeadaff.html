Associates A presentation from the ninth annual SATURN conference held in Minneapolis MN April 29 May 3 2013 Publisher Software Engineering Institute Abstract New NoSQL databases offer more options to the database architect Selecting the right NoSQL database for your project has become a nontrivial task Yet selecting the right database can result in huge cost savings and increased agility This presentation will show how the Architecture Tradeoff Analysis Method ATAM can be applied to objectively select the best database architecture for a project We review the core NoSQL database architecture patterns stores stores graph databases and document databases and then present examples of using quality trees to score business problems with alternative architectures We also address creative ways to use combinations of NoSQL architectures cloud database services and frameworks such as Hadoop HDFS and MapReduce to build solutions that combine low operational costs and horizontal scalability The presentation includes case studies of this process This process is outlined in the book Making Sense of NoSQL published by Manning Publications Share Download PDF Part of a Collection
