2016 The Learning Research and Development Center at the University of Pittsburgh and the Tepper School of Business at Carnegie Mellon University a conference titled Learning in Social Contexts organized by John Levine and Linda Argote in May 2016 The conference brought together leading researchers from several disciplines social and cognitive psychology education organizational behavior strategic management whose work addresses learning processes and outcomes in social contexts The goal of the conference was to highlight current work on both individual and collective learning Although substantial theoretical and empirical work has been done within each of these traditions little effort has been made to bring them together It is hoped that the conference will promote development of a more integrated perspective on learning in and learning by groups and organizations Event Photos 2013 SCALE Conference An interdisciplinary group of researchers see photo met in
