Climate Carnegie Mellon Qatar appoints associate dean for diversity climate August 18 2020 Michael Trick the dean of Carnegie Mellon University in Qatar has created a new leadership role to oversee and guide the campus efforts to become a more diverse inclusive and equitable learning and working community Annette Vincent will be s first associate dean of diversity and climate Vincent joined the faculty in 2012 and has served as program director for Biological Sciences since 2016 In her new role Vincent will provide leadership in planning implementing and evaluating goals related to diversity inclusion climate and equity Trick remarked This new role is integral to the Carnegie Mellon mission of cultivating a diverse world class talent encouraging collaboration and ensuring individuals can achieve their full potential I am very pleased that Professor Vincent will be guiding our community as we continue to grow and Trick is also expanding the responsibilities of the academic area heads a key role that encompasses education curriculum budget program development and faculty hiring The areas heads are Gordon Rule in Biological Sciences Fuad Farooqi in Business Administration Khaled Harras in Computer Science Selma Limam Mansar in Information Systems and Marion Oliver and Dudley Reynolds in Arts and Sciences While some of the area heads are new in their roles all of them have been at Carnegie Mellon University in Qatar for several years said Trick I am proud to see that not only attracts superb educators from around the world but many of our faculty members choose to stay in Qatar to grow their careers and to give back to this academic community through university Farooqi replaces John O Brien who will continue as senior associate dean for faculty and outreach Harras replaces Kemal Oflazer who will remain associate dean of research The Dean s Office also includes senior associate dean for education Selma Limam Mansar associate dean of student affairs and international education Renee Camerlengo and associate dean of government and corporate affairs Fadhel Annan The new leadership team is already tackling their first challenge As the pandemic continues to shift the way universities worldwide are delivering their curricula has opted to begin the academic year in remote learning mode with the possibility of shifting to hybrid mode when it is safe to do so s primary focus is to provide a quality education to our students no matter what This leadership team is seizing the challenge of pandemic restrictions and guiding our entire faculty to adapt
