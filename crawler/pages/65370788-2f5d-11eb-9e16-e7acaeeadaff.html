government In the words of Justice Jackson the rise of the administrative state is likely the most significant legal trend of the last century and perhaps more values today are affected by agency decisions than by those of all the courts This seminar will survey the major ways in which law and political science have grappled with bureaucratic governance How do we understand the rise of the administrative state Why are bureaucracies designed the way they are How do bureaucracies work in the face of legal and political constraints And what avenues are there for meaningful regulatory reform The class is in political science and the law school and course enrollment will be by consent of instructor Students will be responsible for writing short reflection papers and a research paper
